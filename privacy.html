Information We Collect

WebAct may collect information you provide directly and information collected automatically when you use the Service.

Personal Data

While using the Service, WebAct may ask you to provide personally identifiable information that can be used to contact or identify you, including email address, first and last name, phone number, address, state, province, ZIP/postal code, and city.

Usage Data

Usage Data is collected automatically when using the Service and may include IP address, browser type and version, pages visited, time and date of visit, time spent on pages, unique device identifiers, and diagnostic data.

Mobile Device Data

When you access the Service through a mobile device, WebAct may collect the type of mobile device, unique device ID, IP address, mobile operating system, mobile browser type, unique device identifiers, and diagnostic data.

Browser Data

WebAct may collect information that your browser sends whenever you visit the Service or access it through a mobile device.

Data Retention

WebAct retains Personal Data only for as long as necessary for the purposes described in this Privacy Policy, to comply with legal obligations, resolve disputes, and enforce legal agreements and policies.

User accounts are retained for the duration of the account relationship plus up to 24 months after account closure to handle post-termination issues or resolve disputes.

Support tickets and correspondence may be retained up to 24 months from ticket closure. Chat transcripts may be retained up to 24 months for quality assurance and staff training.

Website analytics data, cookies, IP addresses, device identifiers, and server logs may be retained up to 24 months from collection for trend analysis, security monitoring, and troubleshooting.

Longer Retention

Data may be retained longer when required by law, needed for legal claims, requested by you, or present in backup systems scheduled for routine deletion.

When retention periods expire, WebAct deletes or anonymizes Personal Data. Residual encrypted backups may remain for a limited period for security, disaster recovery, or legal compliance.
