Marketing FAQs

Questions businesses ask before starting marketing work.

These answers help explain how the marketing services fit together and where to begin.

Which marketing service should I start with?

If your website is outdated or thin, start by improving the website and core service pages. If your website is strong but people cannot find you locally, start with Local SEO, Google Business Profile, and Local Listings. If you need broader organic growth, start with National SEO. If you want to prepare content for AI search and answer engines, add AI Optimization.

Do I need SEO and Local SEO?

Many businesses benefit from both. SEO helps with broader organic visibility, while Local SEO focuses on nearby customers, map results, local intent searches, service areas, and Google Business Profile strength.

What is AI Optimization?

AI Optimization helps structure content so search engines, AI answer systems, and users can more clearly understand your services, expertise, location, and answers to common buyer questions.

Why are local listings important?

Listings help reinforce business information across directories, map platforms, and local data sources. Consistent information can support trust, discovery, and local visibility.

Can WebAct manage marketing and website updates together?

Yes. That is one of the main benefits of working with WebAct. Marketing improvements often require website updates, new pages, better calls-to-action, landing pages, forms, tracking, or content changes. Keeping those connected makes the process simpler.

How long does marketing take to work?

Some improvements, like profile updates or listing cleanup, can happen quickly. SEO and content growth usually build over time. The timeline depends on your market, competition, website quality, current visibility, and how much ongoing work is being done.
